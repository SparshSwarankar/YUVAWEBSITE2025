Vimarsh 2019

The Vimarshe 2019 conference, themed "Awakened Bharat," was a landmark three-day event held from November 2-4, 2019, at the University of Delhi. The conference successfully integrated rigorous academic discussion with practical, youth-driven initiatives, drawing a remarkable total of 1,484 participants. Attendance included 502 daily participants and 252 volunteers, alongside significant contributions from guests, professors, and field workers. Its broad appeal was evidenced by the participation of students from 77 academic institutions and 18 universities across Delhi and the National Capital Region (NCR). The intellectual core of the conference was exceptionally well-structured, featuring five plenary sessions, two track sessions, and 19 parallel sessions. Thematic discussions spanned national, academic, and social issues. Key sessions on national importance addressed subjects such as Veer Savarkar, the New Education Policy (NEP), the status of Jammu & Kashmir and Ladakh, and Labor Reforms. A notable special session was also dedicated to the topic of "Dara Shikoh: A Nationalist Mughal." The parallel sessions were expanded significantly from the previous year, covering eight distinct domains: Arts, Social Sciences, Languages, Literature, Science, Law, Mass Communication, and UPSC preparation. A major focus of Vimarshe 2019 was the promotion of womenâ€™s empowerment and gender equality. Four dedicated sessions specifically addressed womenâ€™s issues, including their vital role in an "Awakened Bharat" and their representation in the news media. The organizing team achieved its ambitious goal of 50% female participation among the attendees. Furthermore, a groundbreaking precedent was set when the conference was inaugurated with a havan ritual led by a female student, successfully challenging traditional norms. Vimarshe 2019 was also distinguished by its commitment to innovative and sustainable practices. It was a pioneering plastic-free initiative, with all materialsâ€”from food and stationery to gifts and promotional itemsâ€”being entirely devoid of plastic. In an environmentally and culturally mindful move, all drinking water was served in traditional earthen pots. The event also embraced technology by managing 100% of the registration process online, eliminating paper waste. In a notable departure from the norm, speakers, judges, and award winners were honored with books as mementos and prizes, a tribute to knowledge and intellect. The conference's cultural and artistic components were equally vibrant. An exhibition showcased the creative work of student art and photography clubs from five different colleges. The cultural programs featured a diverse mix of performances, including classical and folk dances, musical yoga, and patriotic aerobics. The importance of the event was further underscored by an official tweet from FICCI. In summary, Vimarshe 2019 was a comprehensive success, providing a platform for intellectual discussion while demonstrating a strong commitment to environmental sustainability, gender equality, and cultural preservation, ultimately setting a new standard for student-led conferences.

Vimarsh 2024

Vimarsh 2024, organized by YUVA, brought together diverse voices to discuss key themes including national security, cultural heritage, gender, environment, economy, and holistic development in India. Panel sessions featured distinguished speakers who discussed internal and external threats to India, challenges in the Northeast, cyber security, and the significance of unity and Ekatma Drishti in sustaining the nation. The cultural sessions explored the decolonization of Indian minds, the role of temples, and the impact of cinema on national identity. Gender panels delved into women's historical contributions, the complexities of legal reforms, and transgender experiences in India. Economic discussions emphasized innovation, entrepreneurship, and India's ambitions to become a global manufacturing hub. The environment sessions stressed sustainable development, civic responsibility, and youth engagement in conservation, highlighting success stories and actionable models. The event fostered critical debate, encouraged student participation, and promoted actionable ideas for nation-building. Overall, Vimarsh 2024 served as a platform for intellectual dialogue, inspiring attendees to uphold traditional values while adapting to contemporary challenges, reinforcing the vision of a resilient and inclusive India.
